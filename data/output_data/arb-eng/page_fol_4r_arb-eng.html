The fourteenth chapter [is] about John’s head on the platter, and the five
loaves and two fish for the five thousand men, and Jesus’ walking on the water in the sea
and his censure of Peter when he drowned, and his healing of the mean of maladies in the land of Gennesaret.
The fifteenth chapter [is] about the washing of hands and what comes out of
the mouths of people is what stains them, not what goes into man, and on his expelling the demon
from the girl of the Canaanite woman. And on his healing of the lame and the blind and the mute in his travel through the sea of Galilee.
The sixteenth chapter [is] about the seven loaves and the few fish
from which four thousand men were filled, and about the sign of Jonah the prophet, and his warning regarding him and his statement
whoever wishes to follow the Lord, let him carry his cross. And his statement that among those present some will not taste
death until they see the Son of Man coming in his kingdom. The seventeenth
chapter. When he is revealed on the mountain with Moses and Elijah.
And the voice from heaven speaking to him. And his reply to his disciples that Elijah has already come. He was not
accepted and when he healed the possessed boy, and his saying about faith the measure of the mustard seed,
and about the worldly thing [coin] in the mouth of the fish.
