Matthew from Judea is placed first in order, since he wrote the Gospel in Judea first. He was called out of his tax collecting duties towards God.
Taking up the beginnings of the two lineages of Christ, the one, whose circumcision was first in the flesh, the other, whose election was after the heart, and
Christ is from both lineages, and thus placed in the number of three times fourteen, the beginning extending from the faith to be believed in the time of election and
up to the day of exile as well as leading from the exile all the way to Christ ordaining the coming of the advent of the Lord, he showed
His lineage, so that fulfilling in number and in time and in Himself what He was revealing, and showing the work of God in Himself, and thus he would not deny from the beginning the testimony of the working Christ in these generations whose lineage
he recorded. The time, order, number, method, and reason of all things which is the
pillar of the faith, Christ is God, who, created from a woman, created under the law, born of a virgin, suffered completely in the flesh, was fixed upon the cross, so that, triumphant
over these things in Himself, rising in body He might restore the name of “father” in the fathers to the son and the name of “son” to the father in the children, without beginning or end,
showing Himself to be one with the Father, because He is one. In this Gospel it is useful to those yearning for God to thus know the first things and the middle and the last,
so that they might understand the call of the apostle and the work of the Gospel and the love of God risen in the flesh, reading through all things
and also that they recognize that which in Him, in which they have been understood and desire to understand. For in this zeal we had reason both to pass down the faith of this matter and not to hide the means of the working
God that are to be diligently understood by those seeking it.
